About Us

One of the most iconic breakfast spots in the city, this cafe celebrates the good old days.

In 1932, Narayan Seth bought a small place in the Deccan Gymkhana area that was taken over by Hussain Ali Yakshi, who set up a food joint here in 1935. One of the most popular Irani joints in Pune today, Cafe Goodluck is a culinary gem.


Goodluck Cafe began as a tiny shop. The space earlier served as a cycle repair shop, mechanic shop, and other smaller joints. Though the cafe’s location has not changed, the ambience has. In its early days, Cafe Goodluck had wooden chairs. However, since these were not easily repairable, the cafe switched to plastic chairs. The whole cafe, including the tiles, were damaged in the 1961 floods of Pune. It has three dining halls. As you enter, to your left and right are areas for breakfast. The third one is a narrow lane, lined with chairs and tables, mainly for lunchtime. The waiters come to your table to take and serve the order. The service is very quick.


Cafe Goodluck has a staff of 50–70. Most members of the staff do not come from a restaurant service background. Yakshi has, therefore, had to train them in the business. Whenever a member is absent, he takes their place himself. He maintains a good rapport with his staff.
